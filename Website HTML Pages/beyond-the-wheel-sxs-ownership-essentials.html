The SxS Ownership Guide

Owning a Side-by-Side changes how you ride, how you plan, and how you show up for the people around you. This guide gives every rider—brand new or returning—the foundation to operate, maintain, and handle a machine with clarity, confidence, and respect for real-world trail conditions.

This is not about perfection. It’s about awareness, preparation, habits, and responsibility, so you can ride farther, safer, and with a crew that trusts each other.

What SxS Ownership Really Means

Side-by-Sides are capable, powerful, and unforgiving when approached casually. Ownership means understanding the systems you rely on, the realities of terrain, the limits of design, and the responsibility you have to yourself and your riders.

1. Know Your Machine

Controls, driveline, weak points, maintenance cycles, belts, fluids, and warning indicators. Ownership begins with familiarity—not assumptions.

2. Prepare With Intention

Pre-ride planning, packing logic, weather, timing, radios, route selection, and risk stacking. Good rides start before you ever turn the key.

3. Ride With Structure

Group roles, spacing, pace, stopping culture, clarity signals, and accountability. A structured group is a safe group.

Your First 30 Days of Ownership

The first month sets 90% of your long-term habits. Start small, build awareness, and follow a realistic sequence:

Week 1 — Familiarization

  • Learn controls & seating
  • Review safety features
  • Understand fluids & belts
  • Practice gentle throttle response

Week 2 — Basic Terrain

  • Ride marked, easy trails
  • Practice communication signals
  • Understand momentum & line choice
  • Learn when to stop or reset

Week 3–4 — Real Conditions

  • Introduce mild climbs and descents
  • Build recovery awareness
  • Ride in controlled groups
  • End on confidence, not exhaustion
