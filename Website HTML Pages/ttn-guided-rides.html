A Ride Day with a Plan, Not a Random Convoy.

A TTN™ Guided Ride is a small-group, intentional ride built around clarity, safety, skills and headspace — not just miles. We plan routes, pace, regroup points and expectations around who’s coming, what season they’re in and what they actually need from the day.

You’re not paying to chase dust. You’re stepping into a ride where someone is watching the group, terrain and time windows on purpose — so families, new riders and long-time drivers can all move through the day without guessing.

What You Can Expect Every Time.

  • Pre-ride expectations and route overview before you ever unload.
  • Honest “is this the right day for you?” conversation if needed.
  • Clear structure for line-up, spotters, regroup points and sweep.
  • Space for new riders and future riders to ask “dumb questions” without eye rolls.
  • Post-ride decompression and next-step guidance, not just “see you around.”
ride lanes

Different Seasons. Different Guided Rides.

Not every TTN™ Guided Ride is about the same energy. We use ride lanes so you can land in a group that actually fits your reality — not just whoever clicked the same date.

lane 1

Foundations & First Season Rides.

For brand new riders, cautious spouses, teens stepping into the driver seat and “it’s been a long time” humans who don’t want to be a liability.

  • Basic SxS / ATV handling + trail etiquette woven into the day.
  • Shorter loops, high regroup frequency, low-pressure pace.
  • Perfect for Family & Future Riders crews.
lane 2

Progression & Mixed-Skill Weekends.

For riders who are comfortable on green/blue style terrain and want to grow into more technical features without turning the weekend into an ego contest.

  • Route options that let some push while others stay in a safer loop.
  • Spotter coaching, line choice, recovery basics and “how to read a hill.”
  • Ideal for crews with different confidence levels but shared goals.
lane 3

Reset, Recovery & After-Hard-Season Rides.

For riders, spouses and families coming back after loss, injury, wrecks or burnout who need a day that respects what they’ve walked through.

  • Gentler terrain, shorter windows, heavier focus on headspace.
  • Room to say “I’m done for today” without pressure or shame.
  • Built to rebuild trust between humans, machines and the trail.

What a TTN™ Guided Ride Actually Looks Like.

Every park is different — but our structure stays consistent so your crew always knows what’s happening, why it’s happening and where they fit.

  • 1. Pre-Ride Check-In — We confirm your ride lane, experience range, machines, kids/future riders and non-riding spouses. If a date or park isn’t a good fit, we’ll say so.
  • 2. Staging & Expectations — At the park, we walk through the plan, group roles, hand signals, “how we move as a unit” and non-negotiable safety expectations.
  • 3. First Loop / Warm-Up — Shorter initial loop to gauge pace, comfort and how the group handles terrain. We adjust on purpose — not mid-panic.
  • 4. Main Ride Window — Intentional route choice with planned regroup points, bathroom breaks, fuel checks and honest conversations about fatigue and headspace.
  • 5. Decompression & Debrief — Back at camp or staging, we walk through what went well, what you want to work on and how to pick your next park + lane.
safety & clarity

Non-Negotiables for Every Guided Ride.

  • All machines must be in safe, operable condition.
  • DOT / certified helmets for drivers and passengers.
  • Respect for park rules, locals and landowners.
  • Zero tolerance for substances on ride windows.
  • We reserve the right to reassign, pause or remove anyone from a ride if their choices jeopardize the group, the park or TTN™ standards.

Who TTN™ Guided Rides Are (and Aren’t) For.

this is for you if…

You Care About More Than Just “Sending It.”

  • You’re a new rider or coming back after a hard season.
  • You’re a parent or spouse who wants everyone home safe and still speaking.
  • You want to progress with structure, not just chase the wildest line.
  • You value clear expectations, communication and boundaries on the trail.
  • You’re willing to listen, ask questions and be part of a crew — not the main character.
this is not your lane if…

You’re Looking for Chaos or a Free-For-All.

  • You’re primarily interested in showing off, racing or “one-upping” other riders.
  • You don’t want to follow group structure, pace or regroup points.
  • You’re unwilling to adjust plans for weather, fatigue or safety calls.
  • You’re looking for a party first and a ride second.
  • “Ride. Heal. Belong.™” doesn’t matter to you beyond a decal.

How to Get Into a TTN™ Guided Ride.

We keep ride groups intentionally small, and we build them around real seasons, not just headcounts. That means most TTN™ Guided Rides fill from the inside out — starting with riders and families who are already connected to the Nation.

To get into the mix, start here:

  • 1. Join the Nation so we actually know your story, your rig and your season.
  • 2. Watch the Events & Rides page for posted TTN™ Guided Ride dates and locations.
  • 3. Respond to interest forms early — we’ll match you to the correct lane or suggest a better fit.
  • 4. If you’re a park, shop or small team, you can also host or co-host a Guided Ride as part of the TTN™ ecosystem.
