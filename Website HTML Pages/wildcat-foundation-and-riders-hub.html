Before You Roll Into Wildcat.

Wildcat is incredible — and it’s real. Real climbs, real breakage factor, real variables when weather, traffic and fatigue stack up. This isn’t to scare you. It’s to give you a clear pre-trip checklist so your crew can ride on purpose instead of on luck.

  • Know the current conditions. Check weather, recent rain patterns and official Wildcat updates so you aren’t surprised by mud, closures or park changes.
  • Match the trip to your least-experienced rider. Plan routes, pace and ride windows around the person with the least seat time, not the biggest build.
  • Set “call it” rules before you unload. Decide who can say “we’re done for today” and make sure everyone knows that voice will be respected.
  • Align rigs, radios and recovery. Double-check belts, fluids, tire condition, basic tools and comms — especially if you’re exploring deeper sections of the park.
  • Plan for camp and town, not just trails. Think about fuel, food, bathrooms, sleep and how long you’re actually okay being away from base.

ride culture

How TTN™ Rides Wildcat Differently.

TTN™ isn’t trying to be the loudest rig in the park. We’re building a pattern at Wildcat where clarity, communication and care come first — so people can keep riding, not just keep posting.

structure

Leads, Mid-Group, Sweeps.

We use intentional group structure — especially at a park like Wildcat — so no one is guessing who’s watching which section of the line.

  • Clear leads who know the plan, the park and the group’s limits.
  • Mid-group anchors watching spacing, pace and headspace.
  • Sweeps who treat “getting everyone back to camp” as success, not ego.
communication

Radios, Hand Signals & Honesty.

Wildcat terrain and traffic can change fast. Communication is how we keep people from getting overwhelmed, lost or pushed past their capacity.

  • Basic, shared radio language before wheels move.
  • Simple hand signals when radios fail or batteries die.
  • An expectation that saying “I’m not okay” is a strength, not a burden.
space to grow

Room for New & Nervous Riders.

Wildcat is often where riders test what’s next. We make sure there’s a place for new drivers, returning riders and the ones who are rebuilding after hard chapters.

  • Using easier lines and sections as “classrooms,” not just warm-up.
  • Cheering progression, not pressure, when someone says “not today.”
  • Pairing seat time with real conversations about fear, fatigue and risk.
