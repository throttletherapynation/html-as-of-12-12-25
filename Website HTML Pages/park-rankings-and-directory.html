how this was built

More Than Stars. A System Built Around Real People.

TTN™ rankings aren’t about “best park on the internet.” They’re designed to answer three real questions: Is this a good fit for our season? Do we understand the risks? Can we move through it on purpose instead of guessing?

Each park is scored using the same core criteria so riders can compare apples to apples, not hype to hype. We looked at access, terrain, family range, breakage factor, nearby support and overall ride reality, not just highlight reels.

For the 2025 rankings, we tracked:

  • Park access cost and value (day vs yearly, per machine vs per person).
  • Trail mix and clarity: easy, moderate and hard routes, and how clearly they’re marked and connected.
  • Family-friendly factors: kids, future riders and mixed-skill group comfort.
  • Terrain plan: whether the park fits a day trip, a weekend or a full week.
  • Nearby supplies: fuel, food, parts and town resources within realistic reach.
  • Breakage factor & parts: how hard the terrain is on machines and how quickly you can get back rolling.
  • Camping & lodging: RV/primitive options, nearby Airbnb and overall cost balance.
park lanes

Different Parks. Different Purposes. Clearer Expectations.

Not every park is built for the same kind of rider or season of life. Instead of pretending they are, we use lanes to help you match your crew to the right kind of terrain and energy.

lane 1

Confidence Builder Parks.

Parks where new riders, families and “coming back after a hard season” riders can build skills on purpose.

  • Beginner-friendly routes and clearer navigation.
  • Options for half-day and realistic ride windows.
  • Good fits for TTN™ Guided Rides and family-first days.
lane 2

Progression & Mixed-Skill Terrain.

Parks where you can grow — with a mix of easier loops and more technical options once the crew is ready.

  • Multiple route options from a single basecamp.
  • Places to split the group without losing each other.
  • Ideal for “some ready to push, some rebuilding” groups.
lane 3

Advanced, Technical & High-Commitment.

Parks and zones that ask more of machines, bodies and brains. Not about ego — about honesty.

  • Steeper, tighter, more technical terrain profiles.
  • Higher potential for vehicle damage or recovery needs.
  • Better suited for experienced drivers with clear group structure.

2025 season snapshot

2025 Parks & Rankings.

Riders in the Nation spent the 2025 season grinding seat time, logging hours, testing rigs and sampling terrain across Kentucky, Tennessee and West Virginia. This isn’t “we went once” data — this is how parks actually ride in real life.

Below is the full 2025 ranking set. Use it as a roadmap to match parks to your crew, your skills and your season — then layer on the nationwide directory to plan what’s next.

#1 • TTN™ 2025 Park of the Year
Wildcat Offroad Park — East Bernstadt, KY
Gold Standard Flagship Park
Location: East Bernstadt, KY – Privately owned
Access Cost: Daily and yearly pass options per person
Trails: Mostly easy and moderate, with access to challenging lines
Family Friendly: 10 / 10
Camping / Lodging: RV and primitive camping; Airbnb options nearby; cost is fair
Breakage & Parts: Moderate breakage factor; nearby shops and parts available
Plan For: A full weekend at minimum
Nearby Supplies: Excellent; London and Corbin offer full fuel, food and basics

Wildcat does something rare: it works for almost every rider without watering down the ride. New riders, families, mixed-skill crews and veteran drivers can all build a full weekend here and still want more.

The campground and atmosphere are part of the experience, not an afterthought. For new riders, mixed-skill groups, families and veteran drivers who want a dependable, full-experience weekend, Wildcat Offroad Park is our top recommendation for 2025.

#2
Black Mountain Off-Road (Harlan)
Rugged • Technical
Location: Harlan, KY – County owned
Access Cost: Per vehicle, yearly
Trails: Mostly moderate and hard with some difficult
Family Friendly: 3 / 10
Camping / Lodging: RV & primitive; Airbnb nearby; cost is fair
Breakage & Parts: High breakage risk; limited local parts
Plan For: A weekend
Nearby Supplies: Moderate; gas and basic food in Harlan

Big, steep and unforgiving, Black Mountain is the pick for seasoned drivers with built rigs who want real work and real consequence. Not ideal for new riders or young families, but unforgettable for those chasing climbs, commitment and serious terrain.

#3
Hillbilly Trails
Laid-Back • Family Strong
Location: Pikeville, KY – County owned
Access Cost: Per person, yearly
Trails: Mostly easy and moderate
Family Friendly: 10 / 10
Camping / Lodging: RV & primitive; Airbnb nearby; cost is fair
Breakage & Parts: Low breakage risk; limited local parts
Plan For: A weekend
Nearby Supplies: Excellent; Pikeville has full gas, food and supplies

Scenic, relaxed and easy to stage out of, Hillbilly Trails is perfect for families and mixed-skill groups. Town access is simple, terrain variety is solid and it’s one of the strongest “everyone can go” parks in this 2025 lineup.

#4
Hollerwood Off-Road Park
Moderate Mix
Location: Stanton, KY – County owned
Access Cost: Per person, yearly (from purchase date)
Trails: Mostly moderate with some easy and a few hard
Family Friendly: 7 / 10
Camping / Lodging: RV & primitive; Airbnb nearby; cost is fair
Breakage & Parts: Low breakage risk; limited local parts
Plan For: A day trip
Nearby Supplies: Limited; small-town resources only

Hollerwood brings a scenic, comfortable mix of trails that let you push a bit without feeling on the edge all day. Great for riders leveling up from easy parks or families ready for something more than just “flat and simple.”

#5
TWRA (Tackett Creek)
Remote • Technical
Location: Log Mountain, TN – State owned
Access Cost: Per vehicle, yearly (from purchase date)
Trails: Mostly moderate and hard
Family Friendly: 3 / 10
Camping / Lodging: RV & primitive; Airbnb nearby; cost is fair
Breakage & Parts: Moderate breakage risk; limited local parts
Plan For: A long weekend
Nearby Supplies: Limited; small-town resources only

Tackett Creek is remote, steep and demanding. It’s built for riders who want effort, commitment and rugged lines — not casual cruising. You’ll want experience, a solid crew and spare parts. Not suited for beginners or young families, but a memorable playground for skilled drivers.

#6
Hatfield & McCoy (Pinnacle, Indian Ridge & Pocahontas)
Iconic • Mileage
Location: Northfork, WV – State owned
Access Cost: Per person, calendar year
Trails: Mostly easy and moderate
Family Friendly: 10 / 10
Camping / Lodging: RV & primitive; Airbnb nearby; cost is fair
Breakage & Parts: Low breakage risk; limited local parts
Plan For: A full week
Nearby Supplies: Limited; small-town resources only

Legendary for a reason, these Hatfield & McCoy systems deliver miles of linked trails, big views and a “ride all week” feel. It’s a prime choice for bucket-list trips where you want variety, range and that iconic East Coast trail experience.

#7
TWRA (Royal Blue – Sundquist Unit)
Views • Weekend Flow
Location: Pioneer, TN – State owned
Access Cost: Per vehicle, yearly (from purchase date)
Trails: Mostly easy and moderate
Family Friendly: 10 / 10
Camping / Lodging: RV & primitive; Airbnb nearby; cost is fair
Breakage & Parts: Low breakage risk; limited local parts
Plan For: A long weekend
Nearby Supplies: Limited; small-town resources only

Royal Blue – Sundquist serves up big views, ridge lines and relaxed miles. Trails are approachable for most riders, making it an easy “let’s get away for a few days” destination where you can stack seat time without beating everybody up.

#8
Mine Made Adventure Park
Beginner • Confidence Builder
Location: Vest, KY – County owned
Access Cost: Daily ride fee; free access when lodging on-site
Trails: Mostly easy
Family Friendly: 10 / 10
Camping / Lodging: RV sites, primitive camping & nearby Airbnb
Breakage & Parts: Low breakage risk; limited local resources
Plan For: A weekend
Nearby Supplies: Small-town stores; basic fuel and food

Mine Made is a standout confidence park — excellent for teaching new riders and giving families a calm, low-stress place to learn. Once you’re ready for more technical terrain or faster pace, you’ll naturally graduate up the list.

#9
Leatherwood Off-Road ATV
Calm • Cruise
Location: Leatherwood, KY – Private
Access Cost: Higher per-person fee
Trails: Mostly easy
Family Friendly: 8 / 10
Camping / Lodging: Primitive camping on-site; nearby Airbnb
Breakage & Parts: Limited parts availability
Plan For: A day trip
Nearby Supplies: Small-town fuel and food options

Leatherwood offers an easy-going, low-pressure ride day with gentle terrain and a slower tempo. It’s a nice fit for casual riders and families wanting something mellow; riders searching for tough climbs or technical lines will eventually want to work further up the rankings.
