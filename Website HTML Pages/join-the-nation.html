what it is (and isn’t)

Joining the Nation Is a Commitment, Not a Contract.

You’re not signing away your life, picking a side, or promising to be perfect. Joining TTN means you’re choosing to ride, host, build, or lead with more clarity and care than the world expects — and that you’re willing to be part of a community that does the same.

what it means

You’re choosing how you show up.

  • You value clarity over chaos on rides.
  • You’re willing to learn, ask questions, and grow.
  • You care about getting people home safely.
  • You show respect for land, parks, and crews.
  • You see riders and families as people, not content.
what it doesn’t mean

You don’t have to have it all together.

  • You don’t have to be the most experienced rider.
  • You don’t have to pretend you’re not carrying anything.
  • You don’t have to ride every event or buy every product.
  • You don’t have to agree with everyone on everything.
who this is for

If you care, you’re already part of it.

Riders, spouses, parents, widows, park owners, builders, team members, mentors — anyone who wants to help build a safer, more honest, more connected off-road world has a place here.

Request Guided Ride Registration & Waiver Forms.

This is for riders, families, and groups who are interested in a TTN™ Guided Ride and need the digital Registration & Waiver packet. We review each request to protect riders, parks, and the integrity of the movement before sending any documents.

Guided Ride cost varies based on:

  • Type of ride (family pace, confidence rebuild, technical, etc.).
  • Group vs. individual participation.
  • Typical Guided Ride days (usually Saturday).
  • Non-typical days by request (Sunday–Tuesday or Thursday & Friday).

We’ll review your request and, if it’s a fit, email you the appropriate TTN™ Guided Ride Registration & Waiver forms to complete digitally. Submission of this request does not guarantee a scheduled ride.

ttn programs

Programs Built for the People Behind the Movement.

These programs are how we walk with parks, brands, builders, educators, small businesses, and allies behind the scenes. This page is a high-level overview — full program details, applications, and contracts are only sent after we’ve reviewed your request to protect riders, partners, and the Nation.

partners

Certified & Trusted Partner.

For brands, builders, and service providers whose work directly touches riders and families. Certified & Trusted is about real-world performance, integrity, and how you show up when things get loud, muddy, and emotional — not just what’s printed on a box.

industry sponsor

Industry Sponsor Program.

Built for finance, insurance, transport, recovery, fabrication, media, and other pillars that support the ecosystem. This lane focuses on long-game alignment, realistic education, and impact that actually reaches riders and parks.

community

Community Alliance – Options A & B.

For parks, non-profits, ride series, and local coalitions who see offroad spaces as healing grounds. Option A and B allow different levels of collaboration, from light-touch campaigns to deeper shared initiatives.

mentorship

Mentorship Program.

For individuals or early-stage efforts who don’t yet have a legal business structure, but carry the heart and willingness to grow. Focused on clarity, integrity, and building something that truly serves riders and families.

subsidiary

Subsidiary Program.

For aligned efforts that may live as a TTN™-connected branch or under the wider movement umbrella. This lane is about protecting the mission, language, and IP while giving room for healthy, locally-driven expression.

small business

Small Business Partner – Options A, B & C.

For small businesses with a legal structure who want TTN™ support to grow the right way. Options A, B, and C meet you at different levels of commitment, readiness, and capacity while keeping riders and families at the center of the work.

education

Certified Throttle Therapy Educator.

For guides, instructors, and leaders who want to teach within TTN™ language, standards, and safety culture. This program focuses on clarity, trauma-awareness, and how to lead riders and families through more than just “follow the leader.”

parks

Certified Throttle Therapy Offroad Park.

For parks who want to align with TTN™ mapping, clarity, and culture at a deeper level. This lane focuses on realistic expectations at the gate, safer communication, and a shared standard for what riders walk into when they unload.
