This Was Built So No One Has to Carry It Alone.

The beginning

It Started With Weight No One Could See.

Before there was a Nation, there was a rider and a family trying to keep moving under the kind of emotional and practical weight most people never see. The machines, the trails, and the mountains became a place to breathe when everything else felt too loud or too heavy — but they also exposed how quickly things could go wrong when people were overwhelmed, under-trained, or just trusted the wrong voices.

As rides got bigger, responsibilities grew too: caring for a husband, honoring a marriage, protecting kids and future riders, and carrying commitments to a wider community. There were seasons that should have broken everything. Instead, they became proof that grief, fear, and chaos don’t get the last word when people link arms and decide to build something different.

Never a solo project

Without My Husband, Family & Community, There Is No TTN.

Marriage & Partnership

This Was Built With Him, Not Around Him.

None of this would exist without the support, sacrifice, and belief of my husband. TTN was never an escape from our life together — it was something we chose to build in the middle of it. Every mile, late-night planning session, and hard conversation shaped this Nation as much as any trail or machine ever could.

Family

Family First, Even When the Trail Calls.

The Heart of TTN is about protecting the people we love most while still making space for the parts of us that come alive in the dirt and the dark. Kids, future riders, extended family — they are the “why” behind the standards, the boundaries, and the way we choose to communicate risk and responsibility.

Community

A Nation Built By the Ones Who Showed Up.

Riders, widows, grandparents, park owners, builders, and leaders all poured pieces of their own stories into this. TTN is not one person’s brand; it’s a shared work shaped by people who knew what it felt like to be overwhelmed and didn’t want the next crew to do it alone.

Turning points

Moments That Changed How We Ride, Teach & Lead.

There were rides where the variables stacked up faster than anyone could explain — weather, terrain, machines, group dynamics, unspoken expectations. Those nights didn’t just leave stories; they left a deep conviction that we had to name what was really happening if we wanted people to get home safer.

There were seasons where grief and stress followed us to the mountain. The rides weren’t just “fun trips” — they were lifelines. We started to see clearly how many people were using off-road time to cope with things they never felt safe saying out loud.

And there were moments when the community stepped in — with meals, with machines, with late-night talks, with practical help — proving that this world is at its best when nobody is trying to perform, impress, or pretend they have it all together.

The Heart of TTN Protects How We Build Everything Else.

The story behind TTN is why we are stubborn about safety, honest language, mapping clarity, rider education, and the gear we stand behind. We know what it feels like to be carrying too much, trying to make good decisions with bad information, and hoping nothing goes wrong. The Heart of TTN is the filter we run everything through so we don’t turn people’s pain into content or turn their trust into a sales pitch.
