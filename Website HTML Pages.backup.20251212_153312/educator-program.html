the heart of the program

Education Built Around Humans, Not Just Horsepower.

The TTN™ Educator Program is for the people who do more than point at a line and say “send it.” It’s for those who carry stories, safety, grief, grit, kids, veterans and first-timers while they teach skills on and off the trail.

TTN Educators are trusted, community-centered instructors who help riders understand:

  • New rider basics, machine ownership and pre-ride prep.
  • Group-ride etiquette, communication and trail clarity.
  • Terrain reading, recovery basics and “know your lane” awareness.
  • How to honor mental health, family realities and lived experience in every ride.

The goal isn’t to control your style — it’s to give you a shared language, lens and baseline so riders hear consistent, healing-centered messages across parks, clinics, rides and content.

non-negotiables

What We Expect From Every TTN™ Educator.

When you carry the TTN™ name as an educator, you’re helping shape how riders experience throttle therapy, safety and community. These standards protect you and the people you serve.

Safety First, Not Views First.

Helmets, harnesses, realistic routes, honest trail ratings, and clear “no-go” lines are part of the job. Educators do not pressure riders past their stated limits or medical realities.

Culture Carriers, Not Just Coaches.

You guard tone in the parking lot, at the campsite and online — not just on the trail. Educators honor survivors, families, veterans, women and future riders as people, not props.

Clear Communication & Boundaries.

You communicate with professionalism, set realistic expectations, and are honest about capacity. When something is outside your lane, you say so and redirect instead of winging it.

Every TTN™ Educator commitment is rooted in our mission to help riders Ride. Heal. Belong.™. If an idea or opportunity doesn’t align with that, we slow it down, reshape it — or say no.
