Women of the Nation

“Fearless. Powerful. Unstoppable.”

Women of the Nation is where riders, moms, daughters, caregivers, leaders and quiet fighters all meet on the same ground. You don’t have to fit a stereotype to belong here — you just have to be willing to show up as who you really are, at the pace your story needs.

Whether you’re brand new, coming back after a lot of life, riding for your kids, riding for yourself, or riding so you don’t lose yourself — there’s a lane for you inside TTN™.

who belongs here

If You Carry a Lot and Still Show Up, You’re One of Us.

Women of the Nation isn’t just about who holds the steering wheel. It’s for the women who plan the trip, pack the bags, calm the fears, keep the family moving and still find a way to care for everyone else. This space is built so you have somewhere to breathe, ride and remember you’re more than what you carry.

Riders & Co-Pilots

Drivers, Passengers & Future Drivers.

Some days you’re gripping the wheel. Some days you’re in the passenger seat, reading the terrain and supporting someone else. Both count.

  • New riders learning to take the wheel for the first time.
  • Seasoned riders who finally want their own rig and voice.
  • Women who swap between driver, co-pilot and spotter depending on the day.
Moms, Caregivers & Anchors

The Ones Everyone Depends On.

You may be the one who packs the snacks, double-checks the straps, watches the kids and quietly makes sure everyone comes home safe.

  • Women balancing medical, emotional or family realities with the desire to ride.
  • Moms building “future riders” step by step, one safe experience at a time.
  • Caregivers who need a space where someone else finally looks out for them.
Survivors & Rebuilders

You’re Not “Too Much” for This Community.

Many of our women arrived here with heavy stories — health scares, grief, trauma, burnout, or seasons where life hit harder than expected.

  • You’re allowed to be new, cautious, emotional or tired.
  • You don’t owe anyone your full story to be welcome here.
  • “Showing up” can mean trying on a helmet, riding one easy trail or just being there.

Ride. Heal. Belong.™ — Through a Woman’s Lens.

The TTN™ core pillars don’t change, but the way women experience them often does. This is how Ride. Heal. Belong.™ shows up in the lives, bodies and schedules of women who carry a lot.

for women of the nation
Ride Heal Belong
Ride

A Place to Feel Strong Again.

Riding gives women a space to feel capable, powerful and present — even when the rest of life is loud, demanding or uncertain.

  • Choosing trails and trips that match your real time, energy and health.
  • Learning skills at a pace that respects fear, fatigue and recovery.
  • Riding as yourself, not as the “perfect version” people expect.
Heal

Movement That Meets You Where You Are.

Healing doesn’t always look like “before and after” photos. Sometimes it looks like breathing deeper, laughing once in the middle of a hard week or realizing you’re still here.

  • Using trails and seat time as a release valve, not an escape from responsibility.
  • Honoring pain, fatigue or medical realities instead of hiding them.
  • Letting the trail be a place where your nervous system can exhale.
Belong

You Don’t Have to Earn Your Spot.

TTN™ is built so women don’t have to prove they’re “tough enough” to fit. You belong here because you are human, not because your rig looks a certain way.

  • Riding in groups where “slow down” and “not today” are respected.
  • Finding other women who understand balancing real life with seat time.
  • Being part of a Nation that values who you are, not just what you can endure.
how we build experiences

Built So You Don’t Have to Shrink to Fit In.

A lot of spaces ask women to “just keep up” — in silence. TTN™ is intentionally designed so women can ask questions, adjust the plan and still feel like full members of the crew.

On the Trail

Ride Plans That Respect Real Life.

TTN™ ride culture is built around planning, communication and realistic expectations — especially when women, kids and mixed-skill groups are involved.

  • Pre-ride conversations about pace, terrain, time windows and non-negotiables.
  • Breaks designed around bodies that actually get tired, not just machines that need fuel.
  • Clear roles for leads, co-leads and sweeps so no one is left behind or pressured past their limit.
In the Community

Spaces Where Your Voice Matters.

Whether you’re online or in person, Women of the Nation is built to make room for women’s questions, insights and boundaries.

  • Encouraging women to lead, navigate, spot and teach — not just “come along.”
  • Respecting privacy: you share what you want, when you’re ready.
  • Celebrating wins that don’t look like highlight reels — like loading up and showing up.

You Don’t Have to Do This Alone.

If you’re a woman who wants to ride but feels overwhelmed, intimidated or just plain tired, you are exactly who this community was built for. Start with one small step:

  • Visit New Rider Essentials for a gentle starting point.
  • Join the Nation community and simply watch, read and breathe before you ever post.
  • Talk with your people about a realistic first or next ride, not a perfect one.
gear & identity

Wear What Feels Like You, Not What’s Expected.

Women of the Nation gear is built so you don’t have to pick between “cute but not functional” and “functional but not made with you in mind.” You deserve pieces that respect both your story and your safety.

Comfort & Fit

Your Body, Your Pace.

Different seasons of life mean different needs — curves, scars, medical devices, strength changes and everything in between.

  • Prioritizing pieces that move with you, not against you.
  • Layers that work in real weather, not just in photos.
  • Options that respect modesty, comfort and personal style.
Expression

You’re Allowed to Take Up Space.

Some days you want loud and neon. Some days you want neutral and quiet. Both are valid.

  • Designs that honor strength, softness and everything in between.
  • Graphics that speak to real life, not just highlight reels.
  • Pieces you can wear on and off the trail without feeling like two different people.
Support

You’re Not Just “Merch.” You’re the Movement.

When you support TTN™, you’re fueling education, mapping, safe rides and spaces built for riders who might have otherwise stayed home.

  • A portion of what we build moves back into riders, parks and programs.
  • Your voice helps shape future collections and resources.
  • You become part of a Nation that rides with you, not just in front of you.
