why this exists

Confusion Is a Hidden Risk Factor.

Most riders don’t get in trouble because they’re “bad riders.” They get in trouble because they never had clear information before the ride even started.

Skill level, machine setup and weather all matter — but so does clarity. When trails are poorly marked, vaguely described or mismatched to the crew, riders end up in overbuilt risk for the day they’re having, the rig they’re in and the people they’re responsible for.

real-world problems

More Than a Pretty Map.

A color dot on an app doesn’t always match what the trail actually feels like in real time.

  • “Green” trails that ride like blues in bad weather.
  • “Blue” trails with surprise ledges, shelves or deep ruts.
  • Black trails never meant for stock or family machines.
  • Groups stacked with mixed experience and zero shared expectations.

TTN™ Mapping & Trail Clarity is about matching real riders to real-world trail reality — not just dots on a screen.

missing details

No Distance, No Exit Plan.

Riders deserve to know what it takes to get in and back out.

  • How long the loop really is in time and distance.
  • Whether there’s a bail-out or only a one-way commit.
  • Where you are in relation to camp, fuel and help.
  • What happens if a kid, body or machine taps out early.

Confusion is its own kind of obstacle. This initiative exists to take some of that weight off riders, families, staff and responders.

process

How the Mapping & Clarity System Works.

We’re not guessing. We’re building a repeatable, rider-centered system that parks and Unity Partners can plug into over time — starting at Wildcat Offroad Park.

step one

On-the-Ground Assessment.

Trails are walked, ridden and documented with real conditions — not just old maps or hearsay.

  • Key obstacles, exposure and risk points identified.
  • Weather and season impact considered.
  • Trail flow, choke points and bailout options reviewed.
step two

Owner-Supported & Rider-Verified.

Park owners and TTN™ work together to align on ratings, access and messaging — then riders help verify what’s really happening over time.

  • Owner sign-off on access, gatekeeping and routing.
  • Rider feedback loops for real-time clarity.
  • Updates as trails evolve, erode or change seasonally.
step three

Color-Coded Trail Clarity.

Ratings become less about bragging rights and more about honest expectation-setting.

  • Clear criteria behind green, blue and black labels.
  • Notes for stock, mildly built and heavily built machines.
  • Language that supports riders before they drop in.

What This Produces (Real Deliverables).

This isn’t a vibe. It’s a system. When a park partners into the Mapping & Trail Clarity Initiative, the goal is to produce tools riders can actually use.

  • Trail clarity notes riders understand before they drop in.
  • Aligned naming/numbering so the map matches the ground.
  • Key intersections + “decision points” highlighted.
  • Estimated loop time/distance expectations (where possible).
  • A rider feedback loop so changes don’t go unreported.

what we’re building

A Clarity Lens Parks and Riders Can Share.

This initiative isn’t about perfection — it’s about shared language. TTN is helping develop simple, repeatable ways to talk about trails so riders and parks aren’t guessing in the dark or arguing over colors.

Trail Ratings Mean Something Here.

Color ratings are not bragging rights. They’re communication. TTN pushes for clear criteria so a “green” doesn’t turn into a confidence killer and a “blue” doesn’t become a surprise rescue day.

  • Green: predictable, low-consequence lines, clear bypass options where possible.
  • Blue: consistent technical demand; obstacles may require spotting or commitment.
  • Black: high consequence, sustained difficulty, limited bailout; not for stock/family by default.
mapping

Aligned Maps & Labels.

  • Connecting trail names and numbers to what riders actually see on the ground.
  • Highlighting critical intersections and decision points on the route.
  • Encouraging parks to keep one “source of truth” updated across paper and digital.
difficulty lens

More Than “Green / Blue / Black.”

  • Factoring in exposure, off-camber risk and recovery difficulty.
  • Considering family setups and multi-row rigs, not just single-seater builds.
  • Using TTN™ rankings to show how parks compare in real life, not just marketing.
communication

Plain Language for Real People.

  • Moving away from hype and toward honest, rider-facing descriptions.
  • Helping parks talk to new riders and families, not only experts and locals.
  • Templates and wording parks can adopt without a full marketing overhaul.

How Riders, Parks & Leaders Can Be Part of This.

Trail clarity is a shared responsibility. TTN isn’t here to criticize from the sidelines — we’re here to build solutions with the people actually on the ground.

  • Riders: Share real experiences, near-misses and “we shouldn’t have been here” stories so patterns can be recognized and addressed.
  • Parks: Connect with TTN to explore mapping, communication and safety language support that fits the park you actually run.
  • Leaders & educators: Use the TTN lens when you plan rides, teach skills and set expectations — especially with mixed-skill or family-heavy groups.
